## One-line
The ideal character for the modern age is a flow-driven, truth-telling "jester-hero" who escapes the tax-slave/mind-slave trap and uses social media to fund a self-directed life.

## Key claims
1. Most people are unfree, and it starts young — at ~8, when a child's ambition is met with "that's not realistic" and they internalize a low-agency life.
2. **Flow** is the core trait of the hero and the source of meaning; difficulty done for intrinsic joy is where the happiest, most powerful humans live. See [[concepts/flow]].
3. The hero is a **jester**: games the system's rules to do what he wants, and is the one who names the uncomfortable truth and acts on it.
4. He **navigates chaos and order** — too much chaos = no longer heroic (Rick Sanchez → nihilism); too much order = no longer relatable (Batman → isolation).
5. Freedom requires escaping the **tax slave** (time bartered away) and **mind slave** (attention captured) states. See [[concepts/agency]].
6. The villains: education system, jobs, the social-media loop, purposelessness.
7. The exit runs through specific knowledge ("what would I do forever"), attention reclamation, self-knowledge, and building an audience.
