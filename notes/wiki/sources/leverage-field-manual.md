## One-line
A systems-level operating manual for leverage: **Leverage = Effort × Multiplier**, where the multipliers are Code, Media, Capital, and Judgement — and the modern, permissionless winners are code and media (zero marginal cost, no gatekeeper).

## Key claims
1. **The formula:** Leverage = Effort × Multiplier. Don't work harder — route the same work through multipliers. "The highest-impact change is architecture, not force."
2. **Four multipliers:** Code, Media, Capital, Judgement. Code & Media are *permissionless* (old levers — employees, investors — required permission; new ones need "judgement and a laptop").
3. **Zero marginal cost** is the whole game for code/media. Test: *if your marginal cost to serve the next customer isn't approaching zero, you haven't found your code lever yet.*
4. **Automation rule:** (time saved per occurrence × frequency × 5 years) > build time → automate. Automate the *bottleneck*, not everything. Build software over hiring only when the work is "boring enough to describe."
5. **Buy what doesn't differentiate; build only what compounds your unique advantage.** Build the *platform*, not the product, when you can.
6. **AI as the judgement lever:** "AI turns judgement into code" — encode pattern-recognition/classification/prioritization to run 24/7. It collapsed the skill barrier to code, so the bottleneck is now *knowing what's worth building.* A solo operator + AI ≈ a small company.
7. **Media playbook:** audience-first, own the email list, back-catalogue > virality, 80% evergreen × 5 formats, curate with your framing. → [[concepts/media-as-leverage]].
8. **Capital:** multiplies judgement; compounding at 15% doubles every 4.8 years.
