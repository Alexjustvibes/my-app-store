# Framework (mine): APAG

Type: the human's own framework
Definition source: the writing-coach skill
Use: **full articles and newsletters** (the macro structure). The micro counterpart is [[frameworks/ppp]].

APAG = Attention → Perspective → Advantage → Gamify.

## The four parts (as I use them)
- **Attention** — a hook that uses relevance, appropriate awareness level, and a low effort-to-reward ratio.
- **Perspective** — *paint the enemy*: the common, flawed perspective and the pain it causes; relate with personal experience.
- **Advantage** — *paint the hero*: your credible counter-perspective, backed by examples, social proof, metaphors, quotes.
- **Gamify** — a step-by-step hierarchy of clear, actionable advice that moves the reader from the old perspective to the new outcome.

## How it wires into the rest of the system
- The **Attention** step is governed by [[frameworks/attention-capture-tactics]] and aimed using [[frameworks/levels-of-awareness]].
- **Perspective** (paint the enemy) is exactly the seed structure this wiki runs on — the *overlooked person* + the *common wrong belief* (the "enemy"). The "relate with personal experience" requirement is [[frameworks/the-experience-model]].
- **Advantage / Gamify** map onto the back half of [[frameworks/newsletter-structure]] (Context & Credibility → The List/Framework).
- This is the rubric to diagnose any full draft against (see [[frameworks/content-philosophy]]).
