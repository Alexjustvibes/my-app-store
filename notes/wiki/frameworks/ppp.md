# Framework (mine): PPP

Type: the human's own framework
Definition source: the writing-coach skill
Use: **sections, tweets, and transitions** (the micro structure). The macro counterpart is [[frameworks/apag]].

PPP = Pull → Perspective → Punchline.

## The three parts (as I use them)
- **Pull** — catch attention with a number, claim, or pattern interrupt.
- **Perspective** — a novel take rooted in personal experience or critical thought.
- **Punchline** — a heavy-hitting closer that makes sense of everything above it.

## How it wires into the rest of the system
- **Pull** draws on [[frameworks/attention-capture-tactics]] (numbers, pattern interrupts).
- **Perspective** is the novelty requirement — personal experience ([[frameworks/the-experience-model]]) or original thought, not recycled takes.
- PPP is the unit APAG is built from: a full APAG piece is a sequence of PPP-shaped beats.
- The shipped Notes are essentially PPP at Note scale — e.g., the Discord Note: Pull (the 365k number) → Perspective (you're already a writer) → Punchline ("you're not starting from scratch"). See [[seeds/discord-writing-is-creative-practice]].
