# Framework (mine): Newsletter Structure

Type: the human's own framework
Definition source: the writing-coach skill
Use: the **in-order skeleton for a newsletter** (the long-form delivery of an APAG piece).

## The structure (in order)
1. **Hook / Headline** — written **last**, after the body is done.
2. **Introduction** — a personal anecdote, the story of the problem, a disproven common belief, or a big idea to dissect.
3. **Context & Credibility** — a quote, metaphor, or beginner-level education that sets up the argument.
4. **The List / Framework / One Solution** — the actionable core.
5. **Conclusion / CTA** — a behavior-change prompt or next step.

## How it wires into the rest of the system
- It's [[frameworks/apag]] in long form: Intro = Perspective (paint the enemy), Context & Credibility = Advantage (paint the hero), The List = Gamify.
- "Hook written last" is also the outline-first principle — see [[frameworks/the-outline-system]] (the hook is step 6, after the body exists).
- The Intro's "personal anecdote / disproven common belief" is [[frameworks/the-experience-model]] + the enemy/hero move.
- For this human's pipeline, the newsletter is the stage a validated seed graduates to — e.g., [[seeds/the-writer-is-now-the-content-creator]] (two validated Notes → this structure).
