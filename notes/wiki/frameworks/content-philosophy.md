# Framework (mine): Content Philosophy (the root beliefs)

Type: the human's own framework / philosophy
Definition source: the writing-coach skill ("Core Beliefs")
Use: the foundation all the other writing frameworks grow from.

## Core beliefs (as stated)
- **Humans are emotional beings, not logical.** Content must take readers on an emotional journey. People don't remember what you say — they remember how you make them feel.
- **Attention is the new currency.** Every piece of writing is a transaction: you trade value for the reader's attention, engagement, or action.
- **The writer's unique life experience is the differentiator — not the topic itself.** The topic is the "how." The lived experience is the "why."

## The coaching method these imply (how drafts get diagnosed)
The skill encodes a fixed diagnostic order — the same one used to coach this wiki's Notes:
1. **Hook first** — does it capture attention? Which tactic ([[frameworks/attention-capture-tactics]]) is it using/missing? Right awareness level ([[frameworks/levels-of-awareness]])? If the hook fails, everything else is secondary.
2. **Structure** — run against [[frameworks/apag]] or [[frameworks/newsletter-structure]]; call out missing/weak/out-of-order sections.
3. **Perspective / novelty** — novel take, personal experience, original metaphor — or recycled common knowledge?
4. **Confidence / voice** — is the writer hedging? Quote the weak sentence; name what's weak. Don't soften.
5. **One pointed question** to push deeper.

## The hard rule baked in
**Never write the user's content for them.** Diagnose, question, point the direction. A brief structural example of a principle is allowed; a finished rewrite of their words is not. (This is identical to the CLAUDE.md hard rule against ghostwriting the human's published voice — the wiki and the writing coach agree.)
