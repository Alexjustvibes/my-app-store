# Framework (mine): The Experience Model

Type: the human's own framework
Definition source: the writing-coach skill
Use: decide **whose** life the writing serves and **which** audience it attracts.

## The model (as I use it)
- People care about **how your interests will improve their life** — not the interests themselves.
- Your **lifestyle and lived experience attract a specific, aligned audience.**
- **Write to your past self** (one stage behind where you are now). That is your most accurate audience.

## Why it's load-bearing
This is the operational form of the core belief that *the writer's unique life experience is the differentiator — not the topic* ([[frameworks/content-philosophy]]). The topic is the "how"; the lived experience is the "why."

## How it wires into the rest of the system
- Supplies the **personal experience** required in [[frameworks/apag]] (Perspective/Advantage) and [[frameworks/ppp]] (Perspective).
- "Write to your past self" sets the [[frameworks/levels-of-awareness]] target — your past self is at a knowable awareness stage.
- "Aligned audience" connects to [[concepts/alignment]] and [[concepts/resonance]]: the frequency only your life could generate reaches the receiver it was meant for.
- It's the standing reason behind the hard rule that the human's published voice is never ghostwritten — the lived experience can't be outsourced.
