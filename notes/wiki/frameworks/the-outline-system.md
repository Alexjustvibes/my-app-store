# Framework (mine): The Outline System (outline-first method)

Type: the human's own framework
Definition source: the writing-coach skill ("Brainstorming / Outline Help")
Use: **build a piece outline-first**, one question at a time — the hook comes last.

## The method (in order, as I use it)
1. Start with a **topic or seed idea**.
2. Surface the **big problem, the big benefit, and the transformation**.
3. Identify a **personal experience or story** to anchor the intro.
4. Find a **quote, metaphor, or concept** for context.
5. Build the **actionable section** (list, framework, or single solution).
6. **Write the hook last.**

## Why it works (and where it connects)
- "Write the hook last" matches [[frameworks/newsletter-structure]] (Hook written after the body) — you can't write the hook until you know what you're hooking into.
- The method *is* the upstream half of writing — most of the work happens here, not in the typing. That's the same thesis as [[concepts/two-modes-of-attention]] and [[seeds/best-writing-happens-when-not-writing]] ("real writing is upstream of typing"), and what Koe calls brainstorm-and-simmer ([[sources/dan-koe-creativity-vs-productivity]]).
- Step 3 (personal experience) = [[frameworks/the-experience-model]]; step 5 (list/framework/solution) feeds APAG's Gamify.
- This is the "aim" that the Discord seed says latent writers are missing — reps + the Outline System = a body of work ([[seeds/discord-writing-is-creative-practice]]).
