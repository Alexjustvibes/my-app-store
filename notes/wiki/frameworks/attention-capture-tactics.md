# Framework (mine): Attention Capture & Holding

Type: the human's own framework / toolkit
Definition source: the writing-coach skill
Use: **diagnose whether a hook captures attention, and whether the body holds it.**

## Attention Capture Tactics (diagnose the hook)
Is the hook using any of these?
- Specific numbers
- Pattern interrupts
- Negativity bias (negative framing of a positive message)
- Group callout
- Problem callout
- Potential benefit
- Social proof
- **Confidence and conviction ← most important**
- Active voice
- Warnings and cautionary advice

## Holding Attention (diagnose the body)
- **Structure:** line breaks, bullet lists, short punchy openings, dashes and parentheses over long clauses.
- **Novel perspectives:** metaphors, personal experience, original concepts — not recycled Twitter takes.
- **Curiosity loops:** imply a puzzle with a missing piece; make the reader feel committed to finishing.

## How it wires into the rest of the system
- Drives the **Attention** step of [[frameworks/apag]] and the **Pull** of [[frameworks/ppp]].
- "Confidence and conviction" is also the **voice** check in draft diagnosis — hedging kills it (see [[frameworks/content-philosophy]]).
- Worked example from coaching: the Discord Note leaned on *specific numbers* (365k) + *negativity bias* (you still think your mind wasn't made for writing) + *confidence*. The "writer = content creator" Note leaned on *confidence/conviction* + a *problem callout*.
