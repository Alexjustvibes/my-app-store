# Framework (mine): The Levels of Awareness

Type: the human's own framework
Definition source: the writing-coach skill
Use: **diagnose whether content is aimed at the right reader.**

## The five levels (as I use them)
1. **Unaware** — doesn't know they have a problem.
2. **Problem Aware** — knows something's wrong, not sure what.
3. **Solution Aware** — knows solutions exist, not yours.
4. **Product Aware** — knows your solution, hasn't acted.
5. **Most Aware** — ready to act, needs a nudge.

## Funnel mapping
- **Top-of-funnel (social):** prioritize levels **1–3**.
- **Bottom-of-funnel (newsletters, products):** prioritize levels **3–5**.

## How it wires into the rest of the system
- Sets the **Attention** target in [[frameworks/apag]] ("appropriate awareness level") and the hook target in [[frameworks/attention-capture-tactics]].
- Pairs with [[concepts/media-as-leverage]]: the top-of-funnel social = the audience-building layer (levels 1–3); the newsletter/product = conversion (levels 3–5).
- Diagnostic question I ask of any paragraph: *what awareness level is the reader at when they hit this?*
