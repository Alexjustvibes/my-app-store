# Thinker: Cicero

Roman statesman/philosopher; the wiki's anchor for the classical virtue-and-friendship tradition (and, via otium/negotium, the rest-as-generative thread). Quotes captured in [[sources/commonplace-2026-06-09]].

## Core claims (so far)
- "Friendship can only exist between good men."
- On what "good" means: "those whose actions and lives leave no question as to their honor, purity, equity, and liberality; who are free from greed, lust, and violence; and who have the courage of their convictions." (from *De Amicitia*)

The throughline: **friendship is a moral relationship, not a transactional or affective one.** You cannot be genuine friends with someone whose character you can't trust — virtue is the precondition, not a bonus.
