# Thinker: David Deutsch

Physicist and epistemologist (*The Beginning of Infinity*, *The Fabric of Reality*). The wiki's anchor for **fallibilism**, the primacy of **good explanations**, and an optimistic-but-non-utopian view of progress. Recurring mind per CLAUDE.md; surfaced explicitly in [[sources/ai-reenchantment-alex-2026-03-01]] and underneath [[seeds/working-practice-wrong-explanation]].

## Core claims (as used in this wiki so far)
- **Fallibilism:** all knowledge is conjectural and error-prone; progress comes from error-correction, not certainty. (The backbone of [[seeds/working-practice-wrong-explanation]] — a working practice and a correct explanation are different objects.)
- **Good explanations** are hard to vary — which is why "borrowed" mechanisms attached to a practice are usually bad explanations even when the practice works.
- **Protopia** (the human invokes this via Deutsch): not paradise, but a world that is *incrementally and continuously better*. Used in the article to reframe AI's future as directional-toward-abundance, not dystopia.
- **Creativity is not pattern completion.** The human's anti-AI-flattening argument leans on this: an LLM returns "the statistical shape of what billions would say"; human creativity is "the expression of a specific perspective… developed over a specific life." This is the philosophical floor under [[concepts/resonance]].
