# Thinker: Naval Ravikant

The central modern source for this wiki's economic spine: specific knowledge, leverage, accountability, equity, and long-term games. Most of his claims here come from the "How to Get Rich" / Almanack body of work, captured in [[sources/commonplace-2026-06-09]].

## The manifesto (his compressed version of the whole thing)
> Fast, lift, sprint, stretch, and meditate / Build, sell, write, create, invest and own / Read, reflect, love, seek-truth, and ignore society / Make these habits and say no to everything else / Avoid debt, jail, addiction, disgrace, shortcuts and media / Relax, victory is assured.

## Core claims, grouped
**Specific knowledge** → see [[concepts/specific-knowledge]]
- "Arm yourself with specific knowledge, accountability and leverage."
- "Specific knowledge is found by pursuing your genuine interest rather than whatever is hot right now."
- "Building specific knowledge will feel like play to you but work to others."
- "When specific knowledge is taught it is through apprenticeships not schools."
- "Specific knowledge is often highly technical or creative — it cannot be outsourced or automated."

**Leverage** → see [[concepts/leverage]]
- "Fortunes require leverage. Business leverage comes from capital, people, and products with no marginal cost of replication — code and media."
- "Code and media are permissionless leverage. They're the leverage behind the newly rich. You can create software and media that works while you sleep."
- "An arm of robots is freely available — use it."
- "If you can't code, write books and blogs, record videos and podcasts."
- "Leverage is a force multiplier for your judgement."

**Wealth vs. status** → see [[concepts/wealth-vs-status]]
- "Seek wealth, not money or status. Wealth is having assets that earn while you sleep; money is how we transfer time and wealth."
- "You're not getting rich renting out your time. You must own equity — a piece of a business — to earn your financial freedom."

**Compounding / long-term games** → see [[concepts/compounding]]
- "Play iterated games. All the returns in life — wealth, relationships, knowledge — come from compound interest."
- "Pick an industry where you can play long-term games with long-term people."

**Institutions → individuals** → see [[concepts/institutions-to-individuals]]
- "Don't rely on credibility stamps; we are seeing the transition of power from institutions to individuals."
- "The internet has massively broadened the space of careers — most people haven't figured this out yet."

**Judgement & foundational skills**
- "Judgement requires experience but can be built faster by learning foundational skills."
- "Study microeconomics, game theory, psychology, persuasion, ethics, math and computers."
- "Reading is faster than listening; doing is faster than watching."
- "Learn to sell. Learn to build. If you can do both, you will be unstoppable."

**Authenticity as moat** (connects to FMS positioning — Current Bet)
- "If you are marketing something that's inherently you, nobody can beat you."
- "Ask yourself: is this authentic to me? Is it myself I am projecting?"

**Accountability & focus**
- "Embrace accountability and take business risks under your own name. Society will reward you with responsibility, equity, and leverage."
- "A busy calendar and a busy mind will limit you in this world." (see [[concepts/otium-negotium]])
- "Work as hard as you can — though who you work with and what you work on matter more than how hard you work."
- "Become the best in the world at what you do; keep redefining what you do until this is true."
- "Aliens know the largest limiting factor is ideas."

## Where I diverge
> _(Candidate divergences surfaced at ingest — human to confirm, not asserted.)_
- Naval's frame is overwhelmingly **economic**; the human's interests (otium/negotium, reenchantment, fallibilism) ask what the wealth/leverage is *for*. "Relax, victory is assured" smuggles in a telos Naval never defends.
- "Ignore society / ignore media" sits awkwardly next to a career built on broadcasting to society via media. Worth pressuring.
- Possible divergence on "specific knowledge cannot be automated" in the 2026 AI context — partly true, partly a moving line. (Seed-rich; see shortlist.)
