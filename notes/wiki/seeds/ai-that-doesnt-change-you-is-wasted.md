## Hook (the counterintuitive claim, in one line)
The real risk with AI isn't that it changes you — it's that it doesn't. Most people use the most transformative tool ever built like a calculator: task in, answer out, person unchanged.

## Who this is for (the specific, often-overlooked person)
The heavy, capable AI user who's genuinely more *productive* — drafts faster, ships more, clears the stack — and is, as a person and a thinker, identical to who they were a year ago. They think they're ahead. They're standing still faster.

## The argument
- Two orientations to the same tool ([[concepts/mind-as-scaffolding]], from [[sources/ai-reenchantment-alex-2026-03-01]]): **productivity engine** (transaction — bring it tasks, stay the same) vs. **reenchantment engine** (conversation — bring it *hungers*, get changed).
- The mutation only emerges from the loop: *you want → AI → you want more → new you wants again.* It "retunes your desires" and develops perspective faster than you could alone. Calculator-use never enters the loop, so it never compounds into anything but output.
- The turn / what most people get wrong: they measure AI by *time saved*. The compounding payoff isn't saved time — it's a changed thinker. Optimizing for the first actively forecloses the second (you offload the thinking instead of pressurizing it).
- Connects to [[concepts/resonance]]: the calculator-user can only ever produce convergent, average solutions — "the work that was always going to exist." The loop-user develops the perspective that produces the singular kind.
