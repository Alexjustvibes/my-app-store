# Seed: The most valuable knowledge is structurally un-teachable in a classroom

## Hook (the counterintuitive claim, in one line)
If a skill can be taught in a classroom, that's a sign its value is already falling — the knowledge actually worth having can only be caught, not taught, which is why it's transmitted by apprenticeship and never by school.

## Who this is for (the specific, often-overlooked person)
The person who keeps enrolling — another course, another cohort, another certification — waiting to feel "qualified" before they start. They believe the gap between them and the work is *more instruction*. It's actually *more proximity to someone doing the work*.

## The argument
- Naval: "When specific knowledge is taught, it is through apprenticeships, not schools." (from [[sources/commonplace-2026-06-09]], see [[concepts/specific-knowledge]].)
- The reason isn't institutional failure — it's structural. Specific knowledge is tacit: judgement, taste, the thousand micro-decisions a practitioner can't fully articulate. A classroom can only transmit the *explicit, codified* layer — and anything codifiable is, by that fact, broadly available and declining in value. The classroom systematically can't carry the part that's worth the most.
- The turn / what most people get wrong: they read "apprenticeship" as a medieval relic. But the modern apprenticeship is hiding in plain sight — working in public near people better than you, building in their orbit, getting reps with feedback. The internet made apprenticeship *more* available, not less ([[concepts/institutions-to-individuals]]).
- Pairs with [[seeds/credentials-are-depreciating-assets]]: the credential certifies the codifiable layer (the depreciating one); apprenticeship transfers the tacit layer (the appreciating one). Most people are buying the wrong layer.
- The course-buying loop is often creativity-avoidance in a respectable costume — "I'm not ready" as permission to not start. (Connects to [[seeds/unused-creative-power-is-destructive]].)

## Open question / what would make this undeniable
Needs the "modern apprenticeship" made concrete and non-obvious — what does it actually look like for someone who can't quit and go work for a master? (Building in public? Paid proximity? Replying in the right rooms?) Risk of sounding anti-education; the precise version is "classroom for the codifiable base, apprenticeship for the edge," not "school bad." Best proof: a skill the human acquired by apprenticeship/reps that no course could have given.
