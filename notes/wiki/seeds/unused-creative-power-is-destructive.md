# Seed: Unused creative power doesn't go quiet — it goes toxic

## Hook (the counterintuitive claim, in one line)
The creative urge you keep "responsibly" postponing isn't sitting patiently in storage — it's rotting, and the low-grade misery you can't explain is the smell.

## Who this is for (the specific, often-overlooked person)
The competent professional who is good at their job, stable, "fine" — and quietly, persistently restless. They have a thing they'd make if they had time. They never have time. They don't connect the restlessness to the unmade thing because nothing is obviously *wrong*. Marie-Louise Von Franz says something is.

## The argument
- Von Franz: "One of the most wicked, destructive forces, psychologically speaking, is unused creative power." (from [[sources/commonplace-2026-06-09]]) Note the word — *destructive*, not *wasted*. The framing most people use ("I'll get to it") assumes creative drive is inert when stored. The claim is that it's not inert; it turns on its owner.
- This is the dark mirror of [[concepts/two-modes-of-attention]]: starving the creative mode isn't neutral downtime, it has a cost that shows up as irritability, numbness, contempt for others' work, the 11pm doomscroll.
- The turn / what most people get wrong: they treat creating as a luxury to earn after the responsible stuff is handled. Reframe — creating is *maintenance*. You're not indulging; you're discharging something that damages you when it's pent up.
- Connects to Naval's "ignore society… create" instinct and to [[thinkers/dan-koe]]'s "entrepreneurship is self-improvement in disguise" — making the thing isn't separate from being well, it's a precondition.

## Open question / what would make this undeniable
Need the personal/named hook: a specific person (ideally the human, or a recognizable archetype) whose unexplained restlessness resolved when they started making the thing — before/after. Also: is "destructive" defensible or melodramatic? The piece is stronger if it names the *specific* failure modes pent-up creativity actually takes (contempt, escapism, perfectionism) rather than gesturing at vague malaise.
