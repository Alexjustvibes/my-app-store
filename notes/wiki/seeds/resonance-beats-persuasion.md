## Hook (the counterintuitive claim, in one line)
Every "write better hooks / master persuasion" guide is aiming you at the weaker force. The work that actually moves people doesn't argue them into anything — it makes them feel *recognized*, and you can't fake your way there.

## Who this is for (the specific, often-overlooked person)
The creator grinding copywriting frameworks, hook formulas, and persuasion tactics — optimizing the *argument* — and quietly confused why technically-correct posts still slide off people.

## The argument
- Two different forces: "persuasion moves people through argument; resonance moves them through recognition." In contact with resonant work, people feel something true was named that they knew but couldn't word. From [[concepts/resonance]] / [[sources/ai-reenchantment-alex-2026-03-01]].
- You **cannot manufacture resonance** — no formula produces it. You can only "develop the perspective deep enough" to emit a frequency only you could generate. That's why tactics plateau: they optimize the manufacturable thing.
- Proof from this very wiki: the shipped Discord Note worked on **recognition** ("this is me"), not persuasion — see [[seeds/discord-writing-is-creative-practice]]. The metric we chose to watch for it was literally recognition signals, not argument-winning.
- The turn / what most people get wrong: they treat reach as a *persuasion* problem (better hooks) when it's a *perspective* problem (deeper view). The hook gets attention; only resonance gets remembered and shared.
