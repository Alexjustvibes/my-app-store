## Hook (the counterintuitive claim, in one line)
The most prolific writers of our generation produce thousands of words a day and have never
once thought of themselves as writers — because they do it in Discord servers and Reddit threads.

## Who this is for (the specific, often-overlooked person)
Chronically online 16-25 year olds who consume constantly and have "never written anything,"
but who are, in fact, writing daily — they've just never named it or aimed it.

## The argument
- The daily reps already exist; what's missing is intent and a surface, not skill or talent.
- "Starting from zero" is a false belief that keeps them from starting. They're starting from
  thousands of hours. (Connects to the FMS positioning reframe: *you're not starting from zero.*)
- Reframing the habit they already have as latent creative practice removes the wall.

## Open question / what would make this undeniable
A concrete before/after: one chronically-online person, their Discord output measured, then
pointed at a Note or newsletter. Proof that the reps transfer with almost no ramp.
