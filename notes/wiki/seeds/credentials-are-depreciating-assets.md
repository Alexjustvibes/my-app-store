# Seed: Your credential is depreciating faster than you're earning it

Stage: raw            # raw → validating → newsletter → video
Serves Current Bet: yes
Mission fit (rescored 2026-06-09): CORE — frees people from the credential/institution trap toward economic agency.
Template: overlooked-person + counterintuitive-claim

## Hook (the counterintuitive claim, in one line)
The credential you're grinding years to earn is a depreciating asset — it's worth less the day you finally get it than the day you started, because trust is migrating from institutions to individuals while you wait.

## Who this is for (the specific, often-overlooked person)
The diligent credential-stacker: the person mid-degree, mid-certification, mid-"one more qualification and then I'll be ready," doing exactly what they were told would work — collecting stamps. Not lazy, not foolish. Optimizing hard for a game whose payout is quietly shrinking.

## The argument
- Naval: "Don't rely on credibility stamps; we are seeing the transition of power from institutions to individuals." See [[concepts/institutions-to-individuals]], [[sources/commonplace-2026-06-09]].
- The asset framing is the contrarian lever: nobody thinks of a credential as a thing that *loses* value. But if trust is relocating from the institution to the person, the stamp's purchasing power is falling — and a body of public work + an audience is the *appreciating* asset moving the other way.
- "Most people haven't figured this out yet" (Naval) is the whole opportunity: the lag between the trend being real and being priced in is the window. The credential-stacker is buying high on a declining asset.
- The turn: this is not "credentials are worthless / drop out." It's that **the source of trust changed address.** Where the institution used to vouch for you, now your proof of work does. Putting your reps in public *is* the new credential. (Connects to [[concepts/specific-knowledge]] — apprenticeship over school, [[seeds/apprenticeship-not-school]].)
- Character angle ([[thinkers/cicero]]): when trust comes from the person not the institution, character becomes load-bearing again — a credential can't fake what proof-of-work reveals.

## Open question / what would make this undeniable
Needs a concrete asset-value comparison that lands fast: a named role/field where a public portfolio now out-signals the degree (dev, design, writing are easy; the surprise is somewhere it *shouldn't* be true yet — law? medicine-adjacent? finance?). Risk: reads as tired "skip college" content unless the depreciating-asset framing and the "trust changed address" turn carry it.
