# Concept: Specific Knowledge

Knowledge you can't be trained for — found, not taught in schools. The thing you're so naturally drawn to that building it feels like play to you and looks like work to everyone else.

## The claims (Naval) — see [[thinkers/naval-ravikant]]
- "Specific knowledge is found by pursuing your genuine interest rather than whatever is hot right now."
- "Building specific knowledge will feel like play to you but work to others."
- "When specific knowledge is taught, it is through apprenticeships, not schools."
- "Specific knowledge is often highly technical or creative — it cannot be outsourced or automated."
- "Arm yourself with specific knowledge, accountability, and leverage."

## The human's working version
> _(To confirm.)_ Specific knowledge is the supply side of FMS positioning: the un-copyable thing you bring. It's tacit — built through reps and obsession, surfaced rather than studied. This is why the [[seeds/discord-writing-is-creative-practice]] reframe works: those reps *are* accumulating specific knowledge; the person just hasn't named or aimed it. ("You're not starting from zero.")

## Tensions worth holding
- **The 2026 AI line.** "Cannot be outsourced or automated" is partly a moving target. What's genuinely un-automatable is narrowing — which makes locating *real* specific knowledge more urgent, not less. (Seed-rich.)
- Pairs with [[concepts/leverage]]: specific knowledge is judgement; leverage multiplies it. One without the other underperforms.
