# Concept: Leverage

A force multiplier on judgement. The same good decision applied with leverage produces a vastly larger result — which is why outcomes in a leveraged world are so unequal.

## The claims (Naval) — see [[thinkers/naval-ravikant]]
- "Fortunes require leverage. Business leverage comes from capital, people, and products with no marginal cost of replication — code and media."
- "Code and media are **permissionless** leverage. They're the leverage behind the newly rich — they work while you sleep."
- "Leverage is a force multiplier for your judgement."
- "An arm of robots is freely available — use it."
- "If you can't code, write books and blogs, record videos and podcasts."

## The three forms (and the key split)
1. **Capital** — needs permission (someone must give you money).
2. **Labor / people** — needs permission (someone must agree to work for you).
3. **Code & media** — **permissionless.** No one's approval required. Zero marginal cost of replication.

The whole creator/one-person-business thesis lives in #3. It's the leverage available to anyone, which is exactly why this wiki's pipeline (writing → audience → product) is a leverage play, not a hobby.

## The operating layer (The Leverage Field Manual)
From [[sources/leverage-field-manual]] — the systems view that sits under Naval's three forms:
- **Leverage = Effort × Multiplier.** Don't work harder; route the same work through multipliers. "The highest-impact change is architecture, not force."
- **Four multipliers:** Code, Media, Capital, Judgement. (Code & Media = permissionless; see the split above.)
- **The code-lever test:** if your marginal cost to serve the next customer isn't approaching zero, you haven't found your code lever yet. "Write once, sell forever."
- **Automation rule:** (time saved/occurrence × frequency × 5 yrs) > build time → automate. Automate the *bottleneck*, not everything. Build-over-hire only when the work is "boring enough to describe."
- **Buy what doesn't differentiate; build only what compounds your unique advantage.** Build the *platform*, not the product, when you can.
- **AI as the judgement lever:** "AI turns judgement into code" — and collapsed the skill barrier to code, so the bottleneck is now *knowing what's worth building* (→ [[concepts/mind-as-scaffolding]], [[seeds/the-constraint-was-never-the-code]]).
- The Media multiplier got its own page: [[concepts/media-as-leverage]].
