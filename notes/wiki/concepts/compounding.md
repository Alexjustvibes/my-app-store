# Concept: Compounding / Iterated Games

All durable returns — wealth, relationships, knowledge, audience — come from compound interest, which only pays out to those who keep playing the same game with the same people over a long horizon.

## The claims (Naval) — see [[thinkers/naval-ravikant]]
- "Play iterated games. All the returns in life — wealth, relationships, knowledge — come from compound interest."
- "Pick an industry where you can play long-term games with long-term people."

## The human's working version
> _(To confirm.)_ The pipeline (idea → Notes → newsletter → video) is an iterated game; each published piece compounds with the last. This is also the standing argument *against* the wiki's own failure mode: accumulating inputs without shipping breaks the compounding — you only earn interest on work that's actually published and out playing the game.

## Capital & back-catalogue (Leverage Field Manual)
From [[sources/leverage-field-manual]]:
- **Capital** multiplies judgement: a dollar compounding at 15% doubles every ~4.8 years.
- **Media compounds too:** "your back catalogue works harder than you do" — 300 solid posts beat 5 viral posts and nothing else. Virality is a lottery ticket; the library is the compounding asset (→ [[concepts/media-as-leverage]]). This is the creator-facing case for the shipping-guard discipline.
