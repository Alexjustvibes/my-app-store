# Concept: Institutions → Individuals

The transfer of credibility and power from institutions (degrees, titles, brands, gatekeepers) to individuals with audiences and proof of work. The single most load-bearing macro-trend behind FMS and the creator economy.

## The claims — see [[thinkers/naval-ravikant]]
- "Don't rely on credibility stamps; we are seeing the transition of power from institutions to individuals."
- "The internet has massively broadened the space of careers — most people haven't figured this out yet."

## The human's working version
> _(To confirm.)_ This is the *macro* that makes the *micro* (specific knowledge + permissionless leverage) pay off. Credentials are depreciating assets; an audience and a body of public work are appreciating ones. The lag — "most people haven't figured this out yet" — is the opportunity window, and arguably the urgency behind the whole Current Bet.

## Tensions worth holding
- Power moving to individuals doesn't mean institutions vanish; it means the *source of trust* relocates. Naming where it lands (proof of work, audience, character) is more useful than just declaring institutions dead.
- Connects to Cicero on character ([[thinkers/cicero]]): if trust no longer comes from the institution, it has to come from the person — which puts *character* back at the center of credibility.

## AI accelerant
"Everyone can build software" (when the bar drops to idea-quality — [[concepts/mind-as-scaffolding]]) pours fuel on this trend: more individuals shipping small products for specific people, fewer giant platforms.
