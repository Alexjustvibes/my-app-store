# Concept: Agency (and its two captures: the tax slave & the mind slave)

The capacity to direct your own time, attention, and life toward goals you actually chose. The human's framing: most people live a **low-agency life**, and it's installed early and maintained by two specific captures.

## The human's working version
From [[sources/modern-day-hero-alex-2026-05-24]] / [[frameworks/the-jester-hero]]:
- **Low agency starts young** — around age 8, when ambition meets "that's not realistic" and the child gives up. The standard path (degree → job) then maintains it.
- Freedom = letting go of the prescribed path and directing energy toward self-chosen goals (and the flow that comes with them — [[concepts/flow]]).

## The two captures
- **Tax slave** — has bartered away most of their *time* to someone else's goals, so they can't fund their own pursuits. The tell: they start calling their real ambitions "hobbies" because they can't see time to make them pay.
- **Mind slave** *(his coinage)* — "an individual whose mind is entirely focused on the next dopamine hit, usually their favorite social media platform." The *attention* capture.
- **The double bind:** most tax slaves are also mind slaves — so even the time they reclaim isn't truly theirs. Both time and attention have to be won back.

## How it connects to the economic spine
Agency is the *experiential* side of what Naval frames *economically*:
- Tax slave ≈ "you're not getting rich renting out your time" ([[concepts/wealth-vs-status]]).
- The exit (audience + products via social media) ≈ permissionless [[concepts/leverage]] + [[concepts/institutions-to-individuals]].
- "What would I do forever / always feels positive" ≈ [[concepts/specific-knowledge]].
