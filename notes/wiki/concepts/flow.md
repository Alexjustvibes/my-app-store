# Concept: Flow

Total absorption in a goal-aligned task — the state where the rest of the world fades and attention, task, and goal are unified. The human treats it as the core trait of the [[frameworks/the-jester-hero]] and the engine of meaning.

## The human's working version
From [[sources/modern-day-hero-alex-2026-05-24]]:
- "Meaning is generated from goals. Flow is generated from pursuing them." Flow and purpose are linked — "a person without flow is a person without purpose."
- The conditions: (1) task aligned with your goals, (2) incoming information aligned with the task, (3) full attention on it.
- **Effortful yet effortless** — hard to enter, hard to leave. The payoff is retrospective: even an un-fun, edge-of-ability task becomes "yeah, I really enjoyed that."
- The hero seeks **difficult things he finds fun** — intrinsic motivation against hard problems is where the happiest, most capable people live.

## Lineage
Descends from Csikszentmihalyi's flow (uncited in the article). Worth a thinker page if he becomes recurring; for now noted here.

## Live tension (important)
This framework valorizes being in flow / deep focus **as much as possible** — but [[concepts/two-modes-of-attention]] and [[thinkers/dan-koe]] argue creativity needs the *opposite* state: open-focus rest (DMN), letting ideas simmer *away* from the task. Two readings to resolve:
- They're different stages — flow is for *execution* (TPN), simmering is for *idea generation* (DMN); the article just under-weights the rest half.
- Or they genuinely conflict, and "always in flow" is an overstatement (the article itself admits procrastination + three rewrites — i.e. *return*-to-flow, not permanent flow).
This tension is itself seed-rich.
