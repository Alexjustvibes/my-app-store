# Concept: Wealth vs. Status (and Money)

Three different things people confuse. Wealth is owned assets that earn while you sleep. Money is just the medium that transfers time and wealth. Status is your rank in a social hierarchy — and chasing it is usually a zero-sum trap.

## The claims (Naval) — see [[thinkers/naval-ravikant]]
- "Seek wealth, not money or status. Wealth is having assets that earn while you sleep; money is how we transfer time and wealth."
- "You're not getting rich renting out your time. You must own equity — a piece of a business — to earn your financial freedom."

## The human's working version
> _(To confirm.)_ The operational takeaway is **ownership**: trading time for money caps you; owning equity/assets (incl. permissionless media — see [[concepts/leverage]]) uncaps you. Status games are zero-sum and adversarial; wealth creation is positive-sum. A published body of work is an owned asset, not rented time — which is what makes the pipeline a wealth play rather than a status play.
