# Notes from: The Leverage Field Manual

> Source: the human's notes from an external source ("The Leverage Field Manual").
> Dropped 2026-06-09. Immutable.

The best levers in the modern world are the ones that scale without you: code and media. Write something once, and it replicates at near-zero cost. Build software once, and it serves its millionth user the exact same way it served its first. You don't need to be awake or present.

The old levers required permission. You needed employees or investors or both. The new ones just require judgement and a laptop.

The highest impact change is architecture, not force.

The formula: **Leverage = Effort × Multiplier.**

The multipliers: Code, Media, Capital, Judgement.

The point isn't to work harder, it's to apply the same amount of work through these multipliers.

Zero marginal cost of replication is what makes code and media so good — you can just keep making the same thing for almost no cost. Write once, sell forever.

Useful test: if your marginal cost of serving your customer isn't approaching zero, you haven't found your code lever yet.

Code doesn't just compress distribution. It compresses learning, because you can keep iterating on code faster than anything else.

Time saved per occurrence × how often it happens, stretched over five years. If the result exceeds your build time, automate. Automate the bottleneck, not everything.

Build software instead of hiring, but only when the work is boring enough to describe.

Buy everything that doesn't make you different. Custom software is only an appreciating asset when it compounds your unique advantage.

If you can, build the platform, not the product. A platform solves one class of problems for every customer.

AI turns judgement into code. You can encode pattern recognition, classification, and prioritization into systems that run around the clock. Anywhere you're paying for human judgement applied repeatedly to similar-shaped problems, AI is the lever.

AI collapsed the skill barrier to code itself. The question now is: do you know what's worth building?

A solo operator with AI can now do what previously required a small company — research, writing, customer support, data analysis, design, financial modelling, etc. Before adding headcount for any role, ask whether an AI agent supervised by an existing team member could handle the workload.

AI turns your data into a production asset. You probably already collect data through business; AI changes what data *is* — your documents, frameworks, and historical decisions become inputs that make your workflows smarter over time.

AI removes the old tradeoff between scale and personalization. AI lets you serve every user a different product while keeping a near-zero marginal cost.

Build the audience first, then monetize through products.

Media is a permissionless army that works while you sleep.

Teaching forces clarity. If you cannot explain something simply, you don't understand it well enough. Creating media is a judgement multiplier.

Make 80% of the content evergreen, and publish each piece in five or more formats: audio, video, short clips, newsletter summary, social thread.

Content creation has higher fixed costs (research, thinking, synthesis) and near-zero distribution costs.

Curate, don't just create. A well-curated weekly digest can build as much trust as original analysis, often faster. Curation with your perspective and framing is editorial judgement.

Own an email list so you don't lose your audience.

Your back catalogue works harder than you do. A creator with 300 solid posts and zero virality will almost certainly outperform a creator with 5 viral posts and nothing else.

Media compounds trust and reputation simultaneously. A podcast episode is both a distribution mechanism and a credibility-building mechanism.

Media is a deal-flow magnet. When you post, you become visible to the exact people you want to meet: LPs, investors, potential partners, talented hires, customers.

Audience multiplies your other multipliers.

Capital multiplies judgement at precision through investments. A dollar compounding at 15% doubles every 4.8 years.
