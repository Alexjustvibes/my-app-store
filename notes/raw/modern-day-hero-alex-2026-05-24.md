The average person isn't free.

It starts when you're 8. You have this hobby you really enjoy and you're thinking about doing it for a living — but when you mention it to your family they say it isn't realistic and you immediately give up. Congratulations, you were born into a low agency life.

In the years that followed you started doing what society told you was best. Get a degree, get a job, and your life will be set. But now you feel hopeless because you have no reason to actually keep doing the job — it's just keeping you alive and that's it.

Freedom is possible when you let go of the standard path and really evaluate what you want out of life. The modern-day hero already figured this out.

## Flow — The Main Characteristic of the Modern-Day Hero

A person without flow is a person without purpose.

They're pooling all their attention into things that don't really matter in the grand scheme of things.

They are getting money to live a life that is meaningless.

Meaning is generated from goals. Flow is generated from pursuing them.

The hero with flow pours all their attention, all the information that enters into their consciousness, towards a task that moves their goals forward.

The task that they complete to achieve their goals occupies the totality of their attention.

This way they ALWAYS remain in flow.

That's where they draw the natural power found within.

You achieve flow when the task at hand is aligned with your goals, the information that keeps coming into your consciousness is aligned with the task, and you have all of your attention put into it.

In this state a human being is at their happiest — when the rest of the world fades away and all they are left with is the task that will lead them to achieving whatever goal they set out to achieve.

Even if the task isn't inherently enjoyable in the moment, even if you are pushed to the edges of your concentration, you will look back at whatever task you accomplished and you will say "yeah, I really enjoyed that."

This state of flow is effortful yet effortless — it is difficult to get into but once you are in it you won't want to leave. The taste of joy that you get from it just doesn't compare to anything else. This is how you grow with what gives you joy out of life — instead of forcing yourself to mold into someone you're not, you simply follow what gives you flow.

The modern-day hero naturally taps into this flow state as much as humanly possible throughout their day, because a hero gives their life to a higher personal goal of some kind while pushing their limits against modern-day problems.

So all this is to say the modern-day hero goes around doing difficult things that they find FUN — the difficulty is found in problems that they not only want to solve but enjoy solving.

For example: This article was so fucking difficult for me to create — some of these paragraphs I had to rewrite like three times. Not only that, I kept procrastinating on finishing this article because I knew it would be difficult, but I kept coming back to it.

As soon as I start writing, all of my ideas start flowing out of my mind — it feels fun, I enjoy it. Struggling against the challenges associated with writing an article like this invigorates me. Not only is this article in service to my personal goals, but I find joy in the action of creating it. Intrinsic motivation to do a challenge of great difficulty that pushes you to the edge of your ability is where you will find the happiest, most developed and powerful humans — and that is exactly where the modern-day hero resides.

## The Fun-Maxxing Jester

"Life is one funny mofucka, a true comedian — you gotta love him, you gotta trust him."

The fun-maxxing jester-hero doesn't focus on what he's supposed to do in the system he's in, he just figures out how to do what he wants using the rules in front of him.

The jester really stops giving a fuck about how things are "supposed" to be done — he's come to the realization that reality isn't "supposed" to be taken too seriously because the more you care the more reality will fuck with you and vice versa. When you go with the flow of reality and have fun with it you become more aligned, and the more it will reward you for being in alignment.

Rick Sanchez is a good example of what happens when a fun-maxxing jester character is far too chaotic. He falls into nihilism because although he is doing what he wants whenever he wants there is no greater purpose behind any of it.

There's no journey that he's embarking on, no final destination, nothing to accomplish and achieve. Rick Sanchez as a character feels the weight of this emptiness even though he can't really identify it — as seen when he has depressive episodes or general apathy for the situations he finds himself in.

The Jester-Hero has order. He has a bigger destination in mind for everything he does. He doesn't fall into nihilism because he's genuinely happy — every goal that he achieves in order to get to the destination has a purpose, and the journey that he faces is part of the enjoyment.

The reason why the hero is also a jester is that he's the first person to point out the elephant in the room and do something about it.

He doesn't fall for groupthink. He just sees what truly is and he points it out because he's authentic and indifferent about keeping things civil just for the sake of keeping things civil. He addresses problems and acts on them even if it might make someone unhappy.

He'll look at people having unhappy lives, going to work, returning home, gooning and repeating that cycle and go — you know you can just not do that right? And even though the person may not like it initially, it helps to hear that.

Nobody else will straight up tell someone that because they see problems and think oh, it might make someone uncomfortable if I tell them that. Or oh, that's what MOST people do so it must not be that bad. The Jester-Hero straight up doesn't give a fuck and says it how it is. If you're unhappy with your life do something about it, here's how — instead of being a bitch and pretending like everything is just sunshine and rainbows.

In this sense he causes chaos when he needs to, because sometimes making things less stable is the only way to fix a problem — like if you tear apart a wall to get to the underlying problem of a dead rat within. So too does the jester-hero address the difficult conversations within social scenarios. He knows how to navigate the chaos within that environment and afterward he knows how to bring back order by calming people down and keeping things light.

That's another defining characteristic of the hero — he is able to navigate both chaos and order without falling too much into either.

Fall into chaos and you're no longer heroic.

Fall into order and you're no longer relatable.

People want to relate to the hero. They want to like the person who is protecting them and so does the hero.

What's the point of being a hero if you're going to end up like that nerd Batman socially isolated away in his cave?

The Jester-hero doesn't fall into either category — he can walk between both yin and yang and his authenticity remains undamaged.

The jester-hero's purpose is to enlighten people to a better way of living. But to do that he has to stay free — and staying free in a world designed to take your agency requires a specific kind of structure.

## How the Hero Keeps Himself Free

Enter: The tax slave.

The tax slave is someone who's given up all their agency in service of someone else's goals. They can't put all of their mental energy into the things that they actually want to achieve and remain in flow because they bartered away a majority of their time. It makes it much more difficult to achieve your personal goals. To the point that a majority of people start calling them "hobbies" — because they know there's no way they'll have the time to figure out how to turn them into a sustainable income.

What's even worse is that the same people who are tax slaves may even be mind slaves.

Mind•slave /mīndslāv/ Noun
An individual whose mind is entirely focused on the next dopamine hit usually in the form of their favorite social media platform.
"Hey did you see Henry on his phone like the whole time we were at the bar?"
"Yeah he's such a fucking Mindslave."

The modern-day hero looks at all of this and says "fuck that" — he finds the most accessible opportunity that is the most fun to do and just does that, because remember he doesn't care about what he's "supposed" to be doing.

And the modern-day hero would find that opportunity on social media.

Getting money while working on things that you are passionate about is enabled by social media — you can make an audience showcasing anything you are doing and you can make products that ACTUALLY help your audience.

The only problem is you need to set aside the mental energy to do this.

So you'll have to give up heavy use of social media, maybe even go part time on the job.

Whatever it takes to achieve more flow.

## The Villains of the Modern-Day

You came to this earth to kill demons, of course you have to go through hell

The modern-day hero fights for his own freedom in the day to day and enlightens people to this better way of living. So naturally the main antagonists to the modern-day hero are:

The education system — which was created to make people into workers who live to serve the interests of others.

Jobs — as they keep people comfortable enough to not seek agency anymore.

The social media loop — which removes your time and ability to think independently.

Purposelessness — without purpose you have no reason to seek freedom in the first place.

The world is waiting for more heroes to fight these antagonists — more people teaching others how to find agency in their life, what sources of income are possible outside of a job, and how to find purpose.

## Steps to Start Becoming a Modern-Day Hero

Ask what would I do forever — Out of the hobbies that you have, what could you see yourself doing till the end of your life? What activity always feels positive?

Remove the phone — You cannot live a full life if you spend a majority of it with your attention going 100% towards a device. Learn to spend most of the day without it.

Take a personality test — You cannot be an authentic jester-hero without knowing who you are. Figure out who you are and you can develop yourself much faster.

Build on your strengths — After you figure out who you are you will know what strengths you have. Build on the scaffolding that you already have.

Get on Discord and start telling people your goals — Or wherever you want — but people have to know the journey you're about to go on so they can choose to join you if they wish.

And remember, you can be anything you want to be, you just have to believe in yourself.
